Arctic Snorkeling from Ballstad

-Lofoten Island-

About

Snorkeling is a great way to begin your underwater adventures. It’s your chance to start exploring a new and fascinating world with a minimum of equipment and little training.

You can see beautiful kelp forests, fields of algae, spectacular jellyfish, urchins and starfish, not to mention about different species crabs & fish. Snorkeling is safe, easy and enjoyable for all ages and skill levels. It’s an ideal adventure for families and friends.

Freediving

Lofoten is said to have some of the best freediving spots in the whole of Norway. Rocky terrain, even on the seabed means crystal clear waters. Hundreds of tiny islands in the fjords mean shallow bottoms and habitats for plenty of wildlife.

Wetsuits for this activity fall in the 7+mm range. There are guided tours that offer to take complete beginners out for their first dive and provide all equipment. While it sounds to many like a dangerous activity, we can assure you that freediving in the Arctic is an extremely popular and well-understood sport.

It’s possible to stay in the water for an hour at a time before feeling cold, with the right equipment. Norway is also one of the few countries in the world that allows spearfishing. So if that’s something you’d enjoy, you can catch your own dinner from deep under the water's surface.
